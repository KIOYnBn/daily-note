# How to Learn Grammar
## 1. Master the basics
* Focus on basic word order of sentences
	* Subject + Verb + Object
		* Subject: I, You, We, They, He, She
		* Verb: Action Words
		* Something
	* Note: Not all verb need an object. eg: emotion verb——I cry
- Advenced
		- Subject + `negative` + Verb + Object
		- eg: I don't paly the piano
		- note: Negaive comes after the verb for the verb "to be" and Modal verbs(e.g. can, must, should) 
			- e.g. I am not happy
	- Subject + Verb + Object + `Adverb`
		- How & Where & When
			- How: well, badly, slowly, quickly
			- Where:  In my bedroom, In my office, At home
			- When: On Saturdays, In the morning, At night
		- e.g.: I play the guitar well
	- Subject + Verb + Object + `Adverbial Phrase`
		- Adverbial Phrases of Frequency
			- Fro time to time
			- On a regular basis
			- Now and again
		- e.g. I paly the guitar from time to time
	- Subject + `Adverb Frequency` + Verb
		- Adverbs of frequency (One Word)
			- always, often, never, rarely
		- e.g. I often play the guitar
	- Subject + Auxiliary + Adverb Frequency + Verb
		- e.g. He has never played the guitar

## 2. Learn through speaking
1. DISCOVER new language in model answers, stories or videos
2. PRACTISE with a variety of speaking activities 
	1. Adding words
	2. Subtituting words
	3. changing the tense
	- notes: Sentences must have practical meaning
3. REVIEW with quizzes, stories, and private course podcast
## 3. Learn through examples
1. Using AI to create context
2. Notice SVO(Subject verbs object) word order and adverbs
## 4. Practise with others

# Using correct grammer
The cases of situation
## Talking about the present
Q: Where do you live?
- A
	- Present Simple
		- I live in Spain
	- Present Continuous
		- I am living in Spain at the moment
	- Present Perfect Simple
		- Began in the past and continues now
			- A: I have lived in Spain for five years
	- Present Perfect Continuous
		- Begain in the past and continuous now, focus on activity
			- I have been living in Spain for five years
## Talking about the past
* Q: Have you always  lived in Spain?
* A: 
	* Past simple: 
		* No, I lived in manchester when I was younger
	* Past Continuous: 
		* Telling a sotry with two overlapping events
			* I was living in Manchester when I first met my girlfriend
			* I was working late one night last week, when my colleague asked me if I wanted to go for dinner
	* Past Perfect
		* I was living in Manchester whe I met my girfriend, I had moved there two years earlier
	- Used to: 
		- activity no longer happends
			- I used to live in Manchester
	- Would
		- A habit in the past
			- When I lived in Manchester, I would catch the bus everyday to school
## Talking about the future
Q:  Will yo change your job in the future?
- A:
	* Future with Will
		* A decision you take now
			* I don't know, I haven't thought about that.I think I will carry on with the same job
		* Making a prediction
			* My job's not great but I think it wll get better
	* Future with Going to
		* Something planned or already decided
			* No, I am not going to change my job.
			* I am going to carry on in this job for the next two years
	* Present Continuous
		* Something planned and in your schedule
			* Actually, yes, I am starting a new job on Monday
	* Modals of Possibility
		* Might, Could, May
			* It is possible I will change my job
			* I might change my job

## Compare and Contrast
- Adverbs: really, pretty, rather, quite, totally, strikingly, slightly, distinctly, subtly
### Compare
1. A is similar to B Because
	- London is similar to Paris because they are both capital cities
2. A is similar to B in that
3. They are so alike
	- When it comes to cars, two of the most famous brands are Audi and BMW, they are so alike
4. They are pretty much alike
5. They have a lot in common
6. They are really similar
7. A is as adj as B
	- An Audi is as reliable as A BMW
8. A is identical to B
	- Jack is `identical to` his father
	- Jack is `the same as` his father
	- Jack is `just like` his father
9. Similarly
	- I love football, similarly I am big fan of basketball too
10. Likewise
	- I love Chinese food, likewise, I'm really into Vietnamese food as well
### Constrast
1. A is different from B because(in that)
2. It's adverbs different
	- It's slightly different
3. A is far cry from B
4. A is in a different league from B
5. A is far/way ___ than B
	- Stephen King is far better than Enid Blyton
- `Noun1 + Noun2 + Adverb + connector word`
	1. On the contrary
		- I'm really busy on Mondays, Fridays, On the contray, are much easier.
	2. Conversely
		- I am really busy during the week, but on the weekend, conversely, I have lots of free time
	3. On the other hand
		- I'm busy this week, next week, on the other hand, I have much less work to do

## Express Future
1. Present Continuous when it comes to  future arrangements
	- I am catching the train because I love watching the countryside as I travel.
2. Simple Present when it comes to the timetables/schedules
	- The train leaves On Tuesday at 10am.
3. Will + verb when it comes to the predictions or decisions made now
	- So I'll need to get up early.I think I'll change it to a later train, that's far too early
4. Should + verb
	- A situation that i likely or expected in the future
	- example: I should get to London by 10am Wednesday morning
5. Might / May / Could
	- Possible future events / actions
	- example: I might jump on a train to Manchester
6. If Present, will + verb
	1. If there's enough time, I will do that
7. will be ING
	- An action in progress in the future OR Anexpectation about the future
		- example: It will be raining when I get to London
	- Often used to project yourself into future - looking forward to something
		- example: This time next week, I will be sitting on the beach and reading my favourite book 
8. Will + have + past participle
	- A completed action in the future
		- example: By the end of the week , I will have done a lot of travelling
	- Often used to paln ahead
9. About to + verb: 
	- Describe actions in the immediate future
10. Going to + verb:
	- Future plans and intentions

## Express Present
### Present Simple
1. States: 
	- e.g. I live in Santander
2. Routines:
	- e.g. I go to work by bus
3. Habits:
	- I read the newspaper first thing in the morning
4. Stories:
	- e.g. In this film, this girl travels across America and in the end she finds her family
### Present Continuous
1. State Temporary
	- In contrast of Present Simple( emphasize habit), Present Continuous emphasize temporary
	- e.g. 
		1. I usually drink tea in the morning
		2. Today I am drinking water
		3. I am living in London, with my brother for a few weeks
2. New Habits
	- I am growing some plants at home now
3. Annoying Habits:
	1. My boss is always making me work late
### Present Perfect
1. How Long
	- I have lived here since 2017(a point in time)
	- I have lived here for 3 years(duration)
2. Present Result
	- I have broken my leg
	- `Note`: emphasize the impact at present
3. Experiences
	- I have cooked before
	- `express what new hobby would you like to try`
		- I have never tried parachuting
	- `express places you have visited(or not yet)`
		- I have never been to Paris
	- `Note`: Do not to say "When"
4. Unfinished Time
	- Today, I have eaten a lot
		- Today is the unfinished time
	- I usually play football, but I haven't done any sport this month
### Present Perfect Continuous
1. How Long
	- In contrast with Present Perfect(emphasize on action), Present Perfect Continuous emphasize on time
	- e.g. 
		- I have lived here for 3 years
		- I have been living here for 3 years
2. Temporary Acitivity(a short time)
	- I've been decorating my house
	- I've been learning how to paint
	- I have been watching a lot of TV recently
3. Present Result
	- In contrast with Present Perfect(emphasis action is completed), Present Perfect Continuous emphasize the action
	- e.g. 
		1. The government has been making a lot of mistakes
		2. The government has made 3 big mistakes
